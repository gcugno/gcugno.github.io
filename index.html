About Me



I am an astrophysicist in the Exoplanets and Habitability group at the Swiss Federal Institute for Technology (ETHZ). I will soon move to University of Michigan as an SNF fellow.

My main interests are giant planet formation and evolution. My research focuses on understanding the processes that drive planet formation, identifying the connection between planets and their birth places (protoplanetary disks), as well as determining the chemical composition of atmospheres of young planets.

In addition, I am the Principal Investigator (PI) of a JWST Cycle 1 proposal that was awarded 7.8 hrs of telescope time to image for the first time a forming planet identified by local perturbations of gas kinematic in the disk.
